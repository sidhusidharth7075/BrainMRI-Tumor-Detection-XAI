Tumor Types We Classify

Glioma

Primary brain tumors that originate from glial cells. They can be benign or malignant and are the most common type of primary brain tumor.

Meningioma

Tumors that arise from the meninges, the protective membranes covering the brain and spinal cord. Usually benign but can cause significant symptoms.

No Tumor

Normal brain tissue with no detectable tumor or abnormality. Important for ruling out false positives.

Pituitary

Tumors that develop in the pituitary gland, a small gland at the base of the brain. Can affect hormone production and vision.
